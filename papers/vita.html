RESEARCH INTERESTS

·        Software engineering:

o       Evidence suggests that bugs in simulation software tend to affect sensitivity analyses less than they affect point predictions. To the extent this is true, the implications for practical uses of simulation software are correspondingly significant.

o       Software engineering concepts can be used to assist in pedagogy in a wide assortment of non-software engineering courses, both in computer engineering and electrical engineering. We seek to develop this use of software engineering due to its intrinsic interest and its contemporary relevance to CE and EE programs.

·        Probabilistic analysis:

o       We have developed a method of doing calculations on distribution functions numerically in a way that provides bounds on discretization errors. A primary application aera is risk analysis. We are both developing the technique and software, and applying it to specific problems.

·        Text processing:

o       We are examining use of text processing for mining and version control of Web repositories, for systems that help users interact with information such as sets of documents on the Web.

·        Technology foresight:

o       As a field, technology foresight could benefit from advances in methodology. We propose an approach to doing foresight analyses and evaluating them.
