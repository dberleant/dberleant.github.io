Dependencies may be Unknown
  • Given random variables
  • A and B
  • What is the distribution of
  • Y with samples y=f (a,b)?
  • Two possibilities for A and B
    • They are independent
    • Their dependency relationship is unknown
  • Envelopes in unknown case enclose those in independent case

Accounting for Correlation
  • A way to partially characterize dependency
  • Given two marginals, correlation restricts the space of possible joint distributions
  • Restrictions on joint distributions lead to constriction of CDF envelopes relative to unknown dependency
  • Details of constriction depend greatly on f (.)
  • Other ways to restrict the joint distribution may be useful to investigate (much is unknown)

Making Better Decisions
  • Optimal decisions from specific CDFs
    • Often possible if formula can be found
  • Optimal decisions from CDF envelopes
    • Ambiguity in CDF often leads to
      • a range of possibly-optimal decisions
      • cannot rule out potential optimality of each
    • Heuristic approaches to this problem include:
      • Using midpoint of the range
    • A better approach:
      • Compute monetary value of uncertainty reduction
      • Then, get more information, if benefit > cost
