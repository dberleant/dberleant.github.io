The Approach
Distribution Envelope Determination (DED)
Problem inputs
Arbitrary marginals U and V
A function g(sU,sV)=sX
sU, sV are samples of U, V
sX are samples of derived distribution X
Problem outputs
Envelopes around distribution (cdf) of X
U and V independent  easy, use convolution
U and V have unknown dependency  use DED
U and V have known correlation  use DED
An Example:
p1=(38v2-8v1)/(.08v2+.048v1 )
Notes on Example
Solution is for unspecified dependency
Envelopes would be closer if
v1, v2 have known correlation
Envelopes would be even closer if
v1, v2 are independent
Objective:
Use available information, but
Avoid unsupported assumptions
Envelopes are either sufficient for a decision or demonstrate a need for more information

DED  How it Works
Represent pdfs as discretized marginals
of a discretized joint distribution
expressed in a joint distribution tableau
DED  More on the Tableau
If V and U are independent
Then:   p23=pu2*pv3  (as one of 9 examples)
If V and U have unspecified dependency
Then:   no single value for pij but
p11+p21+p31=pv1,   p12+p22+p32=pv2,   p13+p23+p33=pv3
p11+p12+p13=pu1,   p21+p22+p23=pu2,   p31+p32+p33=pu3
If V and U have correlation value or range
Then:  like for unspecified dependency, but
Correlation implies additional constraints
Getting a Point on
the Left Envelope
Given:
Value x on X-axis, and
A joint distribution tableau (see Figure)
Find the maximum cumulation
for values of X from -∞ to x
Identify contributing pijs
Let  y  be their sum
A point (x,y) on left envelope is now found
Finding Contributing pijs
Every interior tableau cell has:
a pij and an interval (see earlier example)
If the interval low bound is < x
then the pij can be cumulated before x
so, pij contributes
Thus, the cumulation at x is:
The sum of the contributing pijs
Maximizing the Sum of the Contributing pijs
When dependency is unspecified, or
   when correlation value or range is given
The marginals do not determine the pijs
But there are constraints on the pijs
(See earlier slide for examples)
Thus, it is desired to
Maximize a sum given a set of constraints
Use Linear Programming (LP)
Getting All the Points on the Right Envelope
We have obtained one point, so far
The interior cell intervals low bounds
partition the x-axis
m x n tableau has mn interior cells
implying mn interior intervals
implying mn low bounds
implying mn+1 distinct x-axis regions
Hence:
mn+1 points can fully characterize the staircase-shaped right envelope
Computing the Right Envelope
Interior cells with high bounds < x contribute their pijs
(Instead of those with low bounds < x)
Sum of the contributing pijs is minimized
(Instead of maximized)
